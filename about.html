About Me

Photo Placeholder

I'm a mechanical engineering graduate student passionate about medical devices that help improve patients' lives. My father inspired me to pursue this field, and I'm driven by the opportunity to create technology that makes a real difference in healthcare.

My background combines precision engineering, robotics, and hands-on manufacturing experience. Through three internships at Volpi USA, I gained expertise in clinical device development, CAD design, and quality systems. I recently won first place at the Nordic Semiconductor IoT Hackathon for a patient monitoring system.

Currently, I supervise advanced manufacturing operations at Northeastern University's EXP Makerspace, overseeing CNC, waterjet, and welding equipment while training students on technical systems. I'm pursuing opportunities in medical device development where I can apply my mechatronics background to create innovative healthcare solutions.

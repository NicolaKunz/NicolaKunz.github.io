Dual-Mode Gripper for Multi-Scale Object Manipulation

Overview

Designed and prototyped an innovative underactuated robotic hand capable of grasping objects ranging from sub-1mm thin items to larger objects requiring three-point contact. Based on research by Tetsuyou Watanabe, the design features asymmetric finger structures that enable passive mode switching between parallel-grip and turn-over configurations without sensors or complex control algorithms.

Challenge

Manipulation of small-sized objects (less than 1mm thick, under 1 gram) from flat surfaces presents unique challenges‚Äîconventional grippers often flick objects away rather than grasping them. The design needed to handle both extremely small objects and larger items using a single actuator while maintaining simplicity and reliability.

Solution

Developed an asymmetric dual-finger design actuated by a single prismatic joint. The right finger features a sharp scooping tip that slides beneath thin objects and passively rotates via torsion spring to approximately 30 degrees during turn-over mode. The left finger employs a six-bar linkage mechanism that adaptively adjusts its grasping surface based on contact with flat surfaces, rotating to match the scooping finger angle. This configuration addresses the challenge of one finger lifting while the other restrains from above, preventing object ejection.

Technical Details

  • Actuation: Single prismatic joint driving symmetric opening/closing motion
  • Mobility: 1 actuated DOF + 1 passive DOF = 2 total DOF (underactuated system)
  • Right Finger: Scooping mechanism with passive 30¬∞ rotation, torsion spring return, mechanical pin stop
  • Left Finger: Six-bar linkage with slotted prismatic joint for controlled grasping surface rotation
  • System: 15 links, 19 revolute joints, 2 prismatic joints
  • Grasping Modes: Parallel-grip mode (max range 12.767 cm) and turn-over mode (max range 8.486 cm)
  • Manufacturing: 3D printed in PLA with custom bolts, locking washers, and spacers
  • Analysis: Forward kinematics derived for right finger, workspace characterization for both modes

Design Evolution

  1. Hand-sketched initial mechanism concepts and link length calculations
  2. Manual measurements and configuration planning before CAD modeling
  3. First prototype (blue PLA): Revealed fingertip design flaws and excessive joint tolerance
  4. Complete redesign of right finger with optimized geometry
  5. Final prototype (white PLA): Custom 3D printed bolts with cylindrical studs, locking washers, and custom spacers to reduce play and improve rigidity
  6. Iterative refinement achieving reliable grasping performance

Key Innovations

  • Asymmetric finger design enabling functional specialization
  • Passive mode switching through mechanical compliance
  • Single actuator simplicity (reduced cost, no sensors needed)
  • Adaptive grasping without grasp planning algorithms
  • Extended capability beyond original design through beneficial rotation for three-point contact
